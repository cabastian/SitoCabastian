# Idea 1 — Mediterranean Editorial Warmth

**Design Movement:** Contemporary Mediterranean editorial hospitality.

**Core Principles:**
1. Large scenic imagery paired with restrained copy.
2. Warm mineral tones and parchment neutrals instead of generic gradients.
3. Asymmetric content blocks with generous negative space.
4. Tactile surfaces, thin dividers, and understated luxury.

**Color Philosophy:** Terracotta, olive shadow, aged stucco, and cream should evoke a lived-in villa atmosphere. The palette should feel sun-warmed and intimate, reinforcing the emotional promise of a calm stay rather than a high-tech booking product.

**Layout Paradigm:** A layered single-page narrative with an offset hero, alternating split sections, and horizontally expressed room controls. The page should read like a boutique property brochure rather than a centered startup landing page.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin framed buttons with terracotta fills on primary actions.
2. Soft photographic overlays and subtle grain texture.
3. Section labels in spaced small caps above serif headlines.

**Interaction Philosophy:** Interactions should feel calm and precise. Hover states should slightly brighten cards and reveal supporting details without abrupt movement.

**Animation:** Use slow fade-and-rise entrances, gentle image crossfades for room switching, and low-distance hover lifts. Motion should be quiet and hospitality-led, never playful or bouncy.

**Typography System:** Cormorant Garamond for display headlines, Manrope for body and interface labels. Headlines should be elegant and high-contrast; body text should remain compact and legible.

# Idea 2 — Rustic Modern Monolith

**Design Movement:** Rustic modern minimalism with architectural framing.

**Core Principles:**
1. Strong structural sections separated by dark bands and stone-light surfaces.
2. Bold information hierarchy with oversized numerals and concise labels.
3. Room imagery treated as architectural evidence rather than decoration.
4. Dense utility presentation balanced by deliberate empty margins.

**Color Philosophy:** Charcoal, travertine, muted sand, and oxidized bronze should create a more sculptural, serious mood. The emotional effect is confidence, privacy, and permanence.

**Layout Paradigm:** Vertical slabs, edge-aligned content, and offset cards that feel almost print-postered. Avoid symmetric central stacking; use left-heavy compositions with anchored utility panels.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Oversized room numbers and stat callouts.
2. Architectural line dividers.
3. Dark testimonial band with inset review cards.

**Interaction Philosophy:** Controls should feel crisp and mechanical. Switching rooms should resemble moving through architectural plans rather than browsing a soft lifestyle carousel.

**Animation:** Apply masked reveals, horizontal panel shifts, and subtle scale transitions. Motion should feel precise and deliberate, with a slightly cinematic pace.

**Typography System:** Fraunces for headings, IBM Plex Sans for body and UI. Use sharp size contrast and disciplined spacing for a gallery-catalog feel.

# Idea 3 — Friulian Garden Salon

**Design Movement:** Garden salon romanticism interpreted through modern web layout.

**Core Principles:**
1. Soft framing, botanical rhythm, and domestic comfort.
2. Rounded but not overly bubbly geometry, with selective curvature.
3. Photo-led storytelling anchored by intimate content modules.
4. Warm hospitality over overt luxury.

**Color Philosophy:** Sage, linen, faded rose clay, and deep espresso should suggest an inhabited countryside retreat. The palette should feel welcoming and organic, with enough depth to support layered photography.

**Layout Paradigm:** Cascading blocks that step down the page like rooms in a home. Sections should interlock rather than sit in isolated boxes, creating a sense of walking through the property.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Botanical divider motifs.
2. Framed review snippets that resemble handwritten guest cards.
3. Soft-edged gallery panes with shadowed borders.

**Interaction Philosophy:** User actions should feel inviting and domestic. Buttons and tabs should suggest moving from room to room in a guided visit.

**Animation:** Use dissolves, slow parallax, and layered card entrances. Motion should feel airy, like curtains or daylight moving through the house.

**Typography System:** DM Serif Display for headings, Source Sans 3 for body and controls. Use elegant display emphasis with practical supporting typography.
